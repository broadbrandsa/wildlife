# Kruger National Park: Geography, Ecology and Clue System Briefing

**For:** SAWC K9 Unit Mobile Game
**Purpose:** Reference for designing the stylised custom map and the educational clue bank

---

## 1. Geographic and Topographic Overview

### 1.1 Size and extent

Kruger covers approximately 19,485 km² (some sources cite 20,000 km² if adjoining contractual parks like Makuleke are counted). Roughly the size of Wales or Israel. It is 360 km long from north to south, averages 65 km from east to west, with a maximum width of around 90 km. It spans the lowveld of north-eastern South Africa, across Limpopo and Mpumalanga provinces.

### 1.2 Borders and transfrontier connections

The eastern boundary runs along the Lebombo Mountains, forming the international border with Mozambique. The northern boundary is the Limpopo River, which forms the border with Zimbabwe. Kruger is the South African anchor of the Greater Limpopo Transfrontier Park, linking with Limpopo National Park (Mozambique) and Gonarezhou National Park (Zimbabwe).

### 1.3 Altitude and relief

Altitude ranges from approximately 140 m on the eastern plains to around 839 m at Khandizwe, the highest peak. The Lebombo range reaches around 500 m. Scattered granite koppies punctuate the western granite belt. The central and southern areas show the most varied topography; the northern plains are flatter.

### 1.4 Regional character

Kruger is commonly divided into five regions:

- **Far North / Pafuri (Punda Maria to the Limpopo):** Most biodiverse, least visited, most exotic. Riverine forest, fever trees, baobabs, sandstone gorges. Includes Crook's Corner at the Luvuvhu-Limpopo confluence.
- **North / Shingwedzi:** Vast mopane woodlands, big elephant country, the iconic Red Rocks sandstone outcrops.
- **Central / Olifants & Satara:** Open savanna grassland, famous lion sightings, large herbivore herds, the Olifants Gorge. Knob-thorn / marula savanna dominates.
- **South / Skukuza, Lower Sabie, Berg-en-Dal:** Busiest and most developed region, mixed woodland, Sabie River system, highest tourist density.
- **South-west / Pretoriuskop, Malelane:** Highest rainfall, sourveld grasses, granite koppies, sable antelope country.

### 1.5 Gates, camps and infrastructure

Ten main entry gates: Pafuri, Punda Maria, Phalaborwa, Orpen, Paul Kruger, Numbi, Phabeni, Malelane, Crocodile Bridge and Giriyondo (Mozambique border).

Twelve main rest camps: Berg-en-Dal, Crocodile Bridge, Letaba, Lower Sabie, Mopani, Olifants, Orpen, Pretoriuskop, Punda Maria, Satara, Shingwedzi, Skukuza.

Five bushveld camps (more remote): Bateleur, Biyamiti, Shimuwini, Sirheni, Talamati.

Private concessions: Jock, Imbali, Lukimbi, Singita Lebombo, Pafuri/Makuleke.

### 1.6 Main roads

Roughly 1,800 km of roads. The tarred north-south spine is the H1 (Malelane to Pafuri), with linking east-west tar roads: H4-1 (Skukuza-Lower Sabie along the Sabie), H7 (Orpen-Satara), H9 (Phalaborwa-Letaba), H1-7 (Shingwedzi-Pafuri). The S-number gravel loops include the famous S100 (predator hotspot east of Satara) and S28 along the Nsikazi River.

## 2. Rivers and Water Systems

Six major perennial rivers flow east-west across the park toward Mozambique, all rising on the Drakensberg escarpment to the west.

### 2.1 Olifants River

Largest tributary of the Limpopo, approximately 560 km long, rising east of Johannesburg. In Kruger it carves a deep gorge; the views from Olifants rest camp are iconic. Joined by the Letaba near the eastern park boundary, then exits through the Olifants Gorge into Mozambique. Home to tigerfish, bushveld smallmouth yellowfish, dense hippo and crocodile fauna.

### 2.2 Letaba River

Rises in the northern Drakensberg near Tzaneen, enters Kruger west of Phalaborwa, flows past Letaba Rest Camp (Elephant Hall Museum). Engelhard Dam just downstream is a major waterhole.

### 2.3 Luvuvhu River

Rises in the Soutpansberg, flows 200 km north-east, joins the Limpopo at Crook's Corner (meeting point of South Africa, Zimbabwe and Mozambique). Flanked by fever tree forests (Vachellia xanthophloea) in the Makuleke Contractual Park. The single richest birding and botanical site in Kruger.

### 2.4 Shingwedzi River

Rises from the confluence of its Mphongolo and Dzombo tributaries inside the park, flows east through the mopane region past Shingwedzi Rest Camp and Kanniedood Dam, then crosses into Mozambique. Site of the iconic Red Rocks sandstone outcrop.

### 2.5 Sabie River

Rises near Sabie town on the escarpment, flows east past Skukuza and Lower Sabie before entering Mozambique. Supports 49 fish species, making it the most biodiverse river in the park. The H4-1 road along the Sabie between Skukuza and Lower Sabie is considered by many to be the best game-viewing road in Africa. Exceptional leopard density.

### 2.6 Crocodile River

Forms the entire southern boundary of the park, flowing east through Malelane and Komatipoort before joining the Komati.

### 2.7 Timbavati River

Rises west of Kruger, flows east through the Timbavati Private Nature Reserve, enters Kruger south of Orpen, joins the Olifants. Famous historically for white lion sightings.

### 2.8 Smaller rivers

- **Mphongolo** and **Phugwane** in the north feed the Shingwedzi.
- **Tsendze** runs south of Mopani toward the Letaba.
- **Nwaswitsontso** runs through the central north (Satara region).
- **Sweni** runs east from Satara to the Lebombos.
- **Biyamiti, Mbyamiti, Nwaswitshaka** drain the southern granite areas.

### 2.9 Dams, pans and notable waterholes

| Name | Location | Notable for |
|---|---|---|
| Engelhard Dam | Letaba River | Waterbirds, hippos |
| Kanniedood Dam | Shingwedzi River | Birdlife, crocodiles |
| Nsemani Dam | West of Satara | Lion and elephant sightings |
| Sunset Dam | Lower Sabie | Iconic southern view |
| Mlondozi Dam | South-east, Lebombo foothills | Panoramic deck |
| Orpen Dam | Central | Predator sightings |
| Kumana Dam | Between Satara and Tshokwane | Hippo and crocodile |
| Pioneer Dam | Mopani camp | Reflective sunsets |

Seasonal pans (Shilolweni, Mphongolo Loop pans, Mahlangene) fill during the rains and draw huge concentrations of game. SANParks has been decommissioning many artificial boreholes, allowing more natural distribution; old waterhole sites remain as landmarks.

## 3. Geology: The Most Powerful Clue Mechanic

**The fundamental rule: granites dominate the west, basalts dominate the east, rhyolite forms the Lebombos, and sedimentary rocks occur in the far north.**

### 3.1 The granite west

Approximately half of Kruger's surface is granite or gneiss dating back 3.5 billion years (among the oldest rock on Earth), part of the Kaapvaal Craton. Key units:

- **Nelspruit Granite Suite** (southern Kruger: pale grey-pink granite; koppies around Pretoriuskop, Berg-en-Dal, Skukuza, Numbi)
- **Makhutswi Gneiss** (central-west: striped grey-and-white gneiss; Orpen-Satara area)
- **Goudplaats Gneiss** (northern: pale to pinkish gneiss; Mopani to Letaba)
- **Tshombo Granite** (deep north-west near Punda Maria)

Named granite koppies useful for clues: Shirimantanga, Mathekenyane, Nhlanguleni, Shitlhave, Manungu, Mbyamiti, plus many unnamed domes between Pretoriuskop and Skukuza.

### 3.2 The basalt east

Extensive basalt section formed by lava flows over 180 million years ago during the break-up of Gondwana. Underlies the flat, open grassy plains east of the H1 from Tshokwane through Satara to Mopani.

Basalt zone character:
- Dark chocolate to black clayey soils (vertisols, called "turf")
- Sparse tree cover, dominated by knob-thorn and marula
- Open golden grassland after rains
- Predator hotspots: S100, Nwanetsi and Gudzani plains

### 3.3 The Lebombo rhyolite ridge

A tall ridge of rhyolite forms the Lebombo Mountains. Rhyolite is hard and erosion-resistant, creating a prominent eastern border ridge. The Lebombos are an 800 km range stretching from Hluhluwe to the Olifants River, forming the border between Mozambique and South Africa/Eswatini. In Kruger this is a clue-rich zone: rocky, scrubby, cooler at altitude, with Euphorbia confinalis, Lebombo ironwood, klipspringer, Verreaux's eagle.

### 3.4 The far north sandstone (Soutpansberg Group)

Sandstones produce:
- The famous Red Rocks sandstone outcrops on the Shingwedzi River (iron-oxidised, bright red)
- Dimbo koppies near Punda Maria
- Lanner Gorge (cut by the Luvuvhu)
- Thulamela hill (archaeological site)

**Red Rocks viewpoint is literally the answer to your example clue about a red-rocked outcrop near a river in the north.**

### 3.5 Karoo sediments

Bands of shales, sandstones and mudstones between the granite and basalt, particularly in the central-north:
- **Clarens Formation sandstones** (cream-yellow, some caves)
- **Ecca shales** (grey-black, occasionally coal-bearing)
- **Dwyka tillite** (rare, localised)

### 3.6 Dolerite, gabbro and the Phalaborwa complex

Dark igneous intrusions cut older rocks in places. The Phalaborwa complex is a unique carbonatite/alkaline complex famous for copper and phosphate deposits, visible as the massive open-cast mine on the park's western border.

### 3.7 Spatial summary table

| Rock type | Colour | Region | Signature landscape | Key species |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Granite/gneiss | Grey, pinkish-grey | Southwest, west | Rounded koppies, sour grassveld | Sable, reedbuck |
| Basalt | Dark chocolate/black | Central-east plains | Flat open grassland | Lion, cheetah, zebra, wildebeest |
| Rhyolite | Reddish-brown to grey | Lebombo ridge (east) | Steep rocky ridge | Klipspringer, black eagle |
| Sandstone (Red Rocks) | Deep red, iron-stained | Shingwedzi, Pafuri | Weathered outcrops, cliffs | Klipspringer, rock hyrax, Verreaux's eagle |
| Karoo sediments | Cream, grey | Central-north | Low ridges | Mixed woodland |
| Phalaborwa complex | Mixed | Central-west | Visible mine | Not zone-specific |

## 4. Biomes, Ecozones and Vegetation Types

Authoritative framework is Gertenbach's 35 landscapes (1983); for game design, seven main zones work well.

### 4.1 Mopane shrubveld and woodland

Northern plains. Mopane (Colophospermum mopane) leaves are butterfly-shaped, rich in protein, feeding buffalo, impala, elephant, kudu, black rhino. Turn yellow, orange and red in autumn. Occurs north of the Olifants River.

### 4.2 Thorn thickets

Between Olifants and Letaba camps. Mix of knob-thorn, sicklebush and other acacias. The Lonely Bull Trail runs here.

### 4.3 Knob-thorn / marula savanna (central Kruger's signature)

Classic Satara country. Open golden grassland with flat-topped knob-thorns and round-crowned marulas. Large herds of zebra, wildebeest, buffalo, giraffe, impala. Predators follow: lion, leopard, cheetah.

### 4.4 Combretum-Terminalia woodland (south-west)

Between Orpen and Pretoriuskop. Mixed woodland dominated by red bushwillow (Combretum apiculatum), terminalia, spirostachys, sclerocarya (marula).

### 4.5 Sourveld (Pretoriuskop)

Highest rainfall zone. Sour grassland unpalatable to most herbivores. Sable antelope, reedbuck, mountain reedbuck, steenbok. Silver cluster-leaf (Terminalia sericea) is diagnostic.

### 4.6 Riverine / gallery forest

Sycamore fig, jackalberry, Natal mahogany, sausage tree, fever tree line all perennial rivers. Important for breeding birds, kingfishers, eagles, tree squirrels, bushbuck, nyala.

### 4.7 Pafuri sandveld and fever tree forest

At the park's far north, the Luvuvhu flows through fever tree forest alongside baobab and sycamore fig. Nyala, bushbuck, samango monkey, bushpig, Sharpe's grysbok. The Fever Tree Forest is one of the most iconic natural features in Kruger.

### 4.8 Lebombo bushveld

Pafuri has three ecozones: floodplain with acacia and fever tree forest; sandveld with mopane, ironwood and bushwillow; dry shrub mopane and baobab on the Limpopo banks. The Lebombo ridge supports bushwillow, ironwood (Androstachys johnsonii) and Euphorbia confinalis.

### 4.9 Alluvial plain / floodplain grassland

Luvuvhu floodplain has lush grasses and reedbeds with buffalo, elephant, kudu, bushbuck. Similar floodplains on the Limpopo banks near Crook's Corner.

### 4.10 Distinctive botanical features useful for clues

- **Baobab (Adansonia digitata):** Common north of the Olifants, especially around Pafuri and Punda Maria.
- **Fever tree (Vachellia xanthophloea):** Lime-green yellow bark, along Luvuvhu floodplain.
- **Nyala tree / mashatu (Xanthocercis zambesiaca):** Huge evergreen canopies along the Luvuvhu.
- **Lala palm (Hyphaene coriacea):** Pafuri and Crook's Corner.
- **Wild date palm (Phoenix reclinata):** Riverine, especially along Sabie.
- **Sycamore fig (Ficus sycomorus):** Riverbanks park-wide.
- **Tamboti (Spirostachys africana):** South-west woodlands, Orpen area.
- **Silver cluster-leaf (Terminalia sericea):** Pretoriuskop area.
- **Knob-thorn (Senegalia nigrescens) & marula (Sclerocarya birrea):** Central plains.
- **Mopane:** Northern half.
- **Leadwood (Combretum imberbe):** Slow-growing giants along rivers, park-wide.
- **Ilala palm / wild date stands:** Near dams and pans.

### 5.3 Reptiles, fish, amphibians

- Nile crocodile: all perennial rivers; highest density on Olifants, Sabie, Crocodile.
- Rock monitor: rocky koppies.
- Water monitor (leguaan): rivers and dams.
- African rock python: rocky outcrops, riverine.
- Flat lizards (Platysaurus): Red Rocks, Lebombo cliffs, Lanner Gorge.
- Tigerfish: Olifants, Letaba, Luvuvhu, Limpopo.

### 5.4 Seasonality

- **Summer (Nov-Mar):** Full rains, migrant birds arrive (European rollers, Wahlberg's eagles, paradise flycatchers, yellow-billed kites). Rivers full, game dispersed. Baobabs and marulas fruit.
- **Autumn (Apr-May):** Mopane leaves turn yellow/orange/red. Impala rut begins.
- **Winter (Jun-Aug):** Dry, cold nights, clear skies. Rivers shrink, game concentrates at water.
- **Spring (Sep-Oct):** Hot, tension at waterholes. First rains end October. Knob-thorn and weeping boerbean flower.

## 6. Climate and Rainfall Gradient

Strong rainfall gradient from about 750 mm in the south-west (Pretoriuskop) to 400 mm in the north-east.

| Region | Approx annual rainfall | Character |
|---|---|---|
| Pretoriuskop (SW) | 700-750 mm | Wettest, sourveld |
| Skukuza (S) | 550-600 mm | Moderate |
| Satara (C) | 500-550 mm | Savanna |
| Mopani/Letaba | 450-500 mm | Mopane zone |
| Shingwedzi (N) | 400-450 mm | Drier mopane |
| Pafuri (Far N) | 400-500 mm | Rivers bring richer ecology |
| Lebombos (E) | 350-500 mm | Rain shadow |

Prevailing winds: warm easterlies (Indian Ocean) in summer; cooler westerlies and southerly cold fronts in winter. Sunrise in December around 5:05 am, in June around 6:30 am. East-west alignment useful for clue puzzles.

## 7. Human Features and History

### 7.1 Archaeological and pre-colonial sites

- **Thulamela:** Late Iron Age archaeological site on a sandstone hill near Pafuri. Dry-stone walled citadel, Venda heritage. Gold artefacts and 500-year-old royal burials linking it to the Great Zimbabwe and Mapungubwe trade networks.
- **Masorini:** Reconstructed Iron Age village on a koppie east of Phalaborwa Gate. BaPhalaborwa people smelted iron here and traded with coastal peoples.
- **Albasini Ruins:** 19th-century trading post of Portuguese-South African trader João Albasini, near Phabeni Gate.
- **Makahane Ruins:** Fortified stone-walled settlement north of Punda Maria.
- **Rock art:** Pafuri, Bushman Trail, scattered granite koppies (deliberately not precisely marked in public sources).

### 7.2 Colonial and conservation-era features

- **Stevenson-Hamilton Memorial Library** at Skukuza.
- **Shirimantanga Koppie:** James Stevenson-Hamilton's ashes were scattered here with those of his wife Hilda. Stevenson-Hamilton was so respected that local Shangaan people called him "Skukuza" (he who sweeps clean), which is how Skukuza Camp got its name.
- **Jock of the Bushveld route:** Historic 1880s wagon trail through south-west Kruger, commemorated at Afsaal.
- **Crook's Corner:** Historic refuge at the Luvuvhu-Limpopo confluence where fugitives evaded arrest by stepping between South Africa, Zimbabwe and Mozambique.

## 9. Ranger Geography and SAWC K9 Narrative Hook

Kruger is divided into 22 ranger sections, each led by a Section Ranger: Pafuri, Punda Maria, Shingwedzi, Mooiplaas, Vlakteplaas, Shangoni, Mahlangeni, Phalaborwa, Letaba, Olifants, Houtboschrand, Satara, Kingfisherspruit, Tshokwane, Skukuza, Nwaswitshaka, Pretoriuskop, Stolznek, Malelane, Crocodile Bridge, Lower Sabie, Nkuhlu.

The **SAWC K9 Unit** is based at the college near Orpen Gate/Hoedspruit and deploys into the Greater Kruger (plus adjoining reserves: Balule, Timbavati, Klaserie, Umbabat). It pioneered the "free-running pack" of Texan-bred hounds (Black and Tan Coonhounds, Bluetick Coonhounds, Foxhounds) that track poachers through the bush off-leash at up to 40 km/h.

**Game narrative hook:** Players deploy from the SAWC K9 base near Orpen into any of the zones. In-game dog classes map to real roles:
- **Trackers** (Bloodhounds, cold spoor)
- **Apprehension** (Belgian Malinois)
- **Detection** (Springer spaniels, ivory/horn)
- **Free-running pack** (Coonhounds, speed tracking)

## 12. Clue Categories with Worked Examples

Every clue should teach something verifiable so a motivated player can research the answer.

### 12.1 Geological clues

## 13. Eight Game Zones

### Zone 1: Far North (Pafuri / Makuleke / Luvuvhu)
- **Boundaries:** North of the Luvuvhu-Limpopo confluence and north of Punda Maria, to Crook's Corner.
- **Geology:** Soutpansberg sandstone, red-orange outcrops.
- **Vegetation:** Fever tree forest, baobab, nyala-berry, lala palm.
- **Signature species:** Samango monkey, Sharpe's grysbok, suni, nyala, Pel's fishing owl, mottled spinetail, racket-tailed roller, crested guineafowl, African finfoot.
- **Named features:** Pafuri picnic spot, Crook's Corner, Lanner Gorge, Thulamela, Makuleke Contract Park.
- **Signature clue:** "A samango monkey barked from a yellow-barked tree above the confluence of three countries."

### Zone 2: Northern Sandveld & Punda Maria (Mahonie Loop)
- **Boundaries:** South of the Luvuvhu, around Punda Maria and Mahonie Loop.
- **Geology:** Sandveld over sandstone, some granite.
- **Vegetation:** Sandveld mopane, baobabs, cluster-leafs.
- **Signature species:** Eland, roan (occasional), tsessebe, Arnot's chat, racket-tailed roller, three-banded courser.
- **Named features:** Punda Maria rest camp, Mahonie Loop baobabs, Dimbo koppie, Makahane Ruins.
- **Signature clue:** "The dogs found tracks along a loop road named for an ironwood, near a camp whose name means striped donkey."

### Zone 3: Mopane Plains & Shingwedzi (Red Rocks zone)
- **Boundaries:** South of Punda Maria to the Letaba, including Mopani and Shingwedzi rest camps.
- **Geology:** Karoo sediments, Clarens sandstone, Soutpansberg sandstone (Red Rocks).
- **Vegetation:** Mopane woodland and shrubveld, occasional baobab.
- **Signature species:** Elephant (large herds), tsessebe, roan, cheetah (Nshawu), bateleur, Meves's starling, ground hornbill.
- **Named features:** Red Rocks viewpoint, Kanniedood Dam, Shingwedzi, Mopani, Pioneer Dam.
- **Signature clue:** "Iron-red boulders rose above the Shingwedzi where elephants cooled in the river below."

### Zone 4: Letaba & Olifants Gorge
- **Boundaries:** Between Letaba and Olifants rest camps, to the confluence in the east.
- **Geology:** Karoo sediments, Goudplaats Gneiss, basalt encroaching from east.
- **Vegetation:** Mopane transitioning to mixed woodland, riverine along both rivers.
- **Signature species:** Elephant, hippo, crocodile, Pel's fishing owl (lower Letaba), Verreaux's eagle (gorge cliffs), tigerfish.
- **Named features:** Olifants rest camp, Letaba (Elephant Hall), Engelhard Dam, Olifants Gorge.
- **Signature clue:** "From a camp high above a gorge, the K9 team spotted smoke where two rivers finally meet."

### Zone 5: Central Basalt Plains (Satara / Orpen / S100)
- **Boundaries:** Olifants River south to Tshokwane, between Orpen Gate and the Lebombo.
- **Geology:** Basalt, some Karoo sediments.
- **Vegetation:** Knob-thorn / marula savanna, open golden grassland.
- **Signature species:** Lion (highest density), cheetah, wild dog (occasional), kori bustard, lappet-faced vulture, martial eagle, secretary bird, tsessebe.
- **Named features:** Satara, Orpen, Orpen Dam, Nsemani Dam, Kumana Dam, Timbavati picnic spot, S100, Mananga Loop.
- **Signature clue:** "The pride roared on the black basalt plain east of the camp surveyors named Seventeen."

### Zone 6: Lebombo & Nwanetsi (Eastern Ridge)
- **Boundaries:** The rhyolite Lebombo ridge along the Mozambique border.
- **Geology:** Rhyolite, eastern basalt foothills.
- **Vegetation:** Lebombo ironwood, Euphorbia confinalis, bushwillow, rocky grassland.
- **Signature species:** Klipspringer, Verreaux's eagle, Cape vulture (rare), leopard on kopjes, flat lizards, rock hyrax.
- **Named features:** Nwanetsi picnic site, Singita Lebombo concession, Giriyondo border gate.
- **Signature clue:** "Klipspringers whistled on rhyolite boulders as the border fence shimmered in the distance."

### Zone 7: Southern Sabie & Lower Sabie (Leopard Corridor)
- **Boundaries:** Olifants Gorge south to the Crocodile River, Skukuza to Lower Sabie H4-1 corridor.
- **Geology:** Nelspruit Granite Suite, some basalt near Lower Sabie.
- **Vegetation:** Mixed woodland, riverine forest, thicket. Sycamore figs, jackalberry, Natal mahogany.
- **Signature species:** Leopard (very high), lion, elephant, hippo, narina trogon, saddle-billed stork, African finfoot.
- **Named features:** Skukuza, Lower Sabie, Sunset Dam, H4-1, Shirimantanga, Mathekenyane, Tshokwane, Mlondozi Dam, Nkuhlu.
- **Signature clue:** "A leopard dragged her kill into a jackalberry above a dam that glows orange at sunset, near the camp whose name means he who sweeps clean."

### Zone 8: South-West Granite Highlands (Pretoriuskop / Berg-en-Dal / Malelane)
- **Boundaries:** South-west corner, from Pretoriuskop through Berg-en-Dal to Malelane.
- **Geology:** Nelspruit Granite Suite (many koppies).
- **Vegetation:** Sourveld, silver cluster-leaf woodland, granite koppie thicket.
- **Signature species:** Sable antelope (best population in park), mountain reedbuck, reedbuck, oribi (very rare), grey hornbill, black-collared barbet.
- **Named features:** Pretoriuskop (oldest camp), Berg-en-Dal, Malelane Gate, Numbi Gate, Phabeni Gate, Afsaal, Albasini Ruins, Manungu Koppie, Shitlhave Dam.
- **Signature clue:** "Sable bulls grazed under silver-leaf trees in the camp where Jock of the Bushveld once travelled."

### Optional Zone 9 and 10
- **Phalaborwa / Masorini Western Gateway:** Around Phalaborwa Gate, Masorini village, Phalaborwa complex geology.
- **Crocodile River Border:** Thin southern strip, Crocodile Bridge, H4-2, southern fence.

## How to Use This Brief

1. **Map design:** Eight zones become the map's regions with distinct colours, illustrated icons and stylised terrain.
2. **Clue bank:** Structure by category (geology, hydrology, botany, birds, mammals, history, culture, operations, weather, astronomy). Tag each clue with zone and difficulty (zone / feature / specific-spot).
3. **Difficulty curve:** Over a 3-month round, start with zone-level clues (month 1), narrow to feature level (month 2), then specific-spot (month 3, within ~5 km).
4. **Educational layer:** Attach a "Did you know?" fact and a source link to every clue.
5. **Round 1 poacher location shortlist** (for SAWC K9 to select): Red Rocks (Shingwedzi), Lanner Gorge, Crook's Corner, Shirimantanga Koppie, N'wanetsi koppie, Masorini.

### Areas of uncertainty to flag
- Park size varies between 19,485 km² and 20,000 km² depending on whether contractual parks are counted.
- Species ranges shift (wild dog movements, roan and tsessebe populations).
- Rainfall figures are long-term averages.
- Rock art site locations are intentionally vague in public sources; the game should not pinpoint these beyond region level.
- All recommended clue content is drawn from publicly available tourism and scientific sources. Any content referring to specific ranger deployment, real poaching incidents, or specific rhino locations has been deliberately excluded.
